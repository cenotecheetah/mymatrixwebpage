2D Pinhole Camera Parameter Model

üì∑ What Is the Pinhole Camera Model?

The pinhole camera model describes how a 3D point in the world projects onto a 2D image plane through a single point ‚Äî the "pinhole." In 2D, we simplify this to project from a 2D world to a 1D image line.

üßÆ Mathematical Model

Let a point in world coordinates be represented as:

\[ \begin{bmatrix} X \\ Y \end{bmatrix} \]

The projection onto the image plane (1D) is given by:

\[ x = f \cdot \frac{X}{Z} \]

In 2D, we often use a simplified version assuming a single projection axis:

\[ x = f \cdot X \] where:

  • \( f \) is the focal length
  • \( X \) is the coordinate of the object along the axis perpendicular to the image plane
  • \( x \) is the coordinate on the image plane

üìê Intrinsic and Extrinsic Parameters

Intrinsic Parameters refer to the internal camera settings:

  • Focal length \( f \)
  • Principal point \( (c_x, c_y) \)
  • Pixel scaling factors

Extrinsic Parameters relate world coordinates to camera coordinates:

\[ \begin{bmatrix} X_c \\ Y_c \end{bmatrix} = R \cdot \begin{bmatrix} X_w \\ Y_w \end{bmatrix} + T \] where:

  • \( R \) is a rotation matrix
  • \( T \) is a translation vector

üõ†Ô∏è Example Calculation

Given:

  • Focal length \( f = 50 \)
  • World point \( X = 0.2 \)

Then image point \( x \) is:

\[ x = 50 \cdot 0.2 = 10 \]
