2D Matrix and Vector Operations

Vectors

A 2D vector is usually represented as:

\[ \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} \]

Addition:

\[ \vec{a} + \vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix} a_x \\ a_y \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} b_x \\ b_y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a_x + b_x \\ a_y + b_y \end{bmatrix} \]

Scalar Multiplication:

\[ k \vec{v} = k \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} kx \\ ky \end{bmatrix} \]

Matrices

A 2D matrix (2x2) looks like this:

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \]

Matrix Addition:

\[ A + B = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} e & f \\ g & h \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a+e & b+f \\ c+g & d+h \end{bmatrix} \]

Matrix Multiplication:

\[ AB = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} e & f \\ g & h \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} ae + bg & af + bh \\ ce + dg & cf + dh \end{bmatrix} \]

Matrix-Vector Multiplication

\[ A\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} ax + by \\ cx + dy \end{bmatrix} \]

These basic operations are fundamental in computer graphics, physics, and linear algebra applications.
